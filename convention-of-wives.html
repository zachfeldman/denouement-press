THE CONVENTION OF WIVES

THE EVER-EVOLVING MESSINESS OF FRIENDSHIP AND MARRIAGE, AND THE WONDER OF SURVIVAL

A chance encounter leads to a friendship with serious consequences between two women whose lives are unknowingly linked. Dina and Julia, new mothers, initially connect over frustrations with their husbands' demanding surgical schedules. However, years later, as their bond weakens, one questions why; the other keeps a secret. Neither knows that choices made by family members decades earlier have put them on a collision course.

The CONVENTION OF WIVES is a sweeping saga following generations from a shtetl in Odessa to the comforts of Scarsdale, from a rebellion in Glasgow to an affluent life in Boston. The novel's characters bring to life the ever-evolving messiness of friendship and marriage, and the wonder of survival.
