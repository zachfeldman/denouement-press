INDIGENOUS

SEPARATED BY A CENTURY, ONE WOMAN SHAPES HER FUTURE WHILE THE OTHER SEARCHES FOR HER FAMILY'S PAST.

This captivating story was inspired by a true account of the unlikely convergence of strangers from different backgrounds in the West in the 1800s. INDIGENOUS weaves together contemporary and historical threads that explore identity, heritage, connection to traditions and land, and the complex relationships among people as they struggle to find understanding. The novel promises to engage and inspire readers with a slice of America's often-untold history.

Isabel, an Acoma Native American girl, and Samuel, a young Prussian man, meet in 1877 in the heart of the New Mexico Territory and form an unlikely friendship that shapes their futures and those of their peoples.

When an earthquake strikes Berkeley, California, in early 2020, and a vase belonging to Shira's grandparents is uncovered in a cemetery, the former grad student travels to New Mexico to return it and investigate a family mystery. Will what she discovers, whom she meets along the way, and the land she comes to know forever alter her path?

INDIGENOUS will be released on October 27th, 2026, by Denouement Press and will be available on Amazon in softcover and e-book formats.
